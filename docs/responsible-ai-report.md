> **Finding:** The model produces materially higher selection rates and FNR
> disparities for the `<18` cohort (small sample, n=412) vs. `80+`. This is
> expected given clinical risk differences by age, but the **FNR gap for
> `<18` (0.60)** is high in absolute terms — pediatric readmissions may be
> under-flagged. **Recommendation:** consider an age-stratified threshold or
> a separate pediatric model if pediatric volume is significant in production.

## 5. Mitigations Applied

- `scale_pos_weight` / `is_unbalance` tuned to avoid systematic under-prediction
  of the minority (readmitted) class.
- Promotion gate (see [ml-design.md §6](ml-design.md#6-evaluation)) blocks
  registration if equalized-odds difference across `sex` or `ethnicity`
  exceeds 0.10. **The `age_band` disparity above is a documented, accepted
  exception** (clinically expected, monitored — see §3.2).

## 6. Human Oversight & Intended Use

- **Intended use:** Decision-support for care management teams prioritizing
  post-discharge outreach. **Not** intended for use in coverage, billing, or
  any adverse clinical/administrative determination.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** All `high` risk-tier patients are reviewed by a care
  manager before any intervention is scheduled.
- **Model card distribution:** This report is attached to the model registry
  entry and must be reviewed by the Clinical AI Governance Committee prior to
  any `prod` deployment.

## 7. Monitoring Commitments

- Fairness metrics are recomputed on every retraining cycle (`cd-ml.yml`).
- Production data drift and prediction-distribution drift are monitored via
  [`src/monitoring/`](../src/monitoring) (see
  [runbook-operations.md](runbook-operations.md)).
- Any drift alert that correlates with a fairness metric regression triggers
  an automatic rollback per the runbook.
